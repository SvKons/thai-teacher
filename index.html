Frequently asked questions

  • How long does it take to start speaking Thai? I want to start speaking Thai as soon as possible! How long does it usually take for your students to start talking?

    +
    It depends on your level, the number of lessons, and how much you practice on your own. Most of my students can confidently use basic phrases and understand simple dialogues within 1-2 months.

  • Is your course suitable for beginners? I have no knowledge of Thai at all. Do you teach complete beginners, or do I need some basic skills?

    +
    Of course! The course is designed for everyone, even if you have never studied Thai before. I explain the basics step by step, help with pronunciation, and provide effective memorization techniques.

  • How are the lessons structured? What is a typical lesson like? Do we focus more on speaking, grammar, or exercises?

    +
    We practice a lot of speaking, go through grammar, work on pronunciation, and listen to real Thai speech. I tailor the materials to your level and goals.

  • Can I take lessons at a convenient time? My schedule is unpredictable. Can I adjust the lesson times to fit my routine?

    +
    Yes, the schedule is flexible, and we can find a time that works best for you. If your schedule changes, we can always reschedule lessons.

  • Do you give homework? I want to practice between lessons. Will I have any homework?

    +
    Yes, but it won’t be boring exercises! I give engaging tasks that help you learn faster—listening to Thai, repeating phrases, watching videos, or doing small speaking challenges.

  • Will I be able to travel in Thailand without problems after your course? I want to visit Thailand and feel confident communicating in shops, cafes, and on the streets. Will I be able to do that after your course?

    +
    Absolutely! I teach practical, real-life Thai that will help you navigate cities, order food, ask for directions, and interact with locals.

  • Do you teach only online, or are in-person lessons available? Can we meet in person for lessons, or are they only online?

    +
    Right now, I only teach online, but it’s actually more convenient—you can learn from anywhere, and all materials are easily accessible.
